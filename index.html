The Ratty Drink Kiosk

I observed real users interacting with a public interface at Brown University, The Beloved and Infamous Ratty Drink Kiosk, to create UX personas and storyboards. Is it a usable and efficient interface?

The Interface.

The Ratty Drink Kiosk is a beverage dispenser that allows users to select a myriad of drink options with the convenience of one centralized source. As seen to the right, at the initial screen, users can select their drink of choice by clicking on a bubble icon. The user will then be directed to a second screen where, if applicable, they can customize the flavor of their drink. For instance, a user can select Minute Maid Lemonades and then will be directed to a second screen where they can choose strawberry flavoring to create strawberry lemonade.

The user can also return to the initial screen using the back button on the top left corner. In the unfortunate– and quite frequent– case that the kiosk runs out of a specific beverage, the corresponding bubble with the grayed-out indicates its unavailability. Below the terminal, the physical ice and drink dispenser lever can be pushed back by users to fill their cup with their desired ratios. There is an additional contact-free option available where users can scan the QR Code on the top left of the home screen and select the same options from their phone.
